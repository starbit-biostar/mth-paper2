# MTH Paper 2: Ancient Astronomical Calendars and Transgenerational Reproductive Timing

**Exploratory Evidence for Cultural Calibration**

Ulysses Angulo¹, Maria Sheretova²

¹ Biostar Technology Research Institute, Czech Republic  
² Independent Researcher, Czech Republic

## Summary

This paper investigates whether cultures with stronger ancient astronomical traditions show transgenerational reproductive timing more precisely aligned with astronomical periods.

### Key Results

- **ρ = 0.733** — Cultural TW modes correlate with ancient astronomical site density (N = 9 groups, 95% CI [0.15, 0.95])
- **3 × Metonic = 55.8 yr** vs TW mean 55.0 yr (**1.5% deviation**)
- **3 × Saros = 54.1 yr** vs TW mean 55.0 yr (**1.7% deviation**)
- **3 × Jupiter-Saturn = 59.6 yr** vs Middle Eastern TW mode 60.3 yr (**1.2% deviation**)
- **Testable prediction**: migrant populations should show TW drift toward host culture's mode
